# Nutrition & Musculation Skill

Use this skill for TDEE/macro calculations, training program design, nutrition planning, periodization, supplementation, and strength/hypertrophy optimization.

## TDEE & BMR Calculation

### Formulas (Choose Based on Context)

**Mifflin-St Jeor (Recommended Default - 82% accuracy)**
- **Men**: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) + 5
- **Women**: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161

**Katch-McArdle (Best for Lean/Muscular)**
- BMR = 370 + (21.6 × Lean Body Mass_kg)
- LBM = Weight_kg × (1 − bodyfat% / 100)
- Requires knowing body fat percentage

**Harris-Benedict Revised (Cross-Reference)**
- **Men**: BMR = 88.362 + (13.397 × kg) + (4.799 × cm) − (5.677 × age)
- **Women**: BMR = 447.593 + (9.247 × kg) + (3.098 × cm) − (4.330 × age)

### Activity Multipliers

| Level | Multiplier | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Sedentary | ×1.2 | Desk job, minimal exercise |
| Lightly active | ×1.375 | Light exercise 1-3 days/week |
| Moderately active | ×1.55 | Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week |
| Very active | ×1.725 | Hard exercise 6-7 days/week |
| Extra active | ×1.9 | Very hard exercise + physical job |

**TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier**

**All calculators are ±10% accurate — adjust based on real-world results after 2-3 weeks.**

## Macro Calculation from TDEE

### Bulking (Muscle Gain)
- **Surplus**: +10-20% above TDEE (~250-500 kcal)
- **Protein**: 1.6-2.2 g/kg/day
- **Fat**: 0.5-1.5 g/kg (20-35% of calories)
- **Carbs**: Fill remainder (≥3-5 g/kg)
- **Target gain**: 0.25-0.5% bodyweight/week

### Cutting (Fat Loss)
- **Deficit**: −20-25% below TDEE
- **Protein**: 2.3-3.1 g/kg of lean body mass (critical for muscle preservation)
- **Fat**: 15-30% of calories (minimum 0.5 g/kg for hormones)
- **Carbs**: Fill remainder
- **Target loss**: 0.5-1% bodyweight/week

### Maintenance / Recomposition
- **Calories**: At TDEE or slight deficit (0-10% below)
- **Protein**: 2.0-2.4 g/kg/day
- **Fat**: 25-35% of calories
- **Carbs**: Fill remainder

### Example: 80 kg, 3200 kcal Lean Bulk
- **Protein**: 2.0 g/kg × 80 = 160 g (640 kcal)
- **Fat**: 1.0 g/kg × 80 = 80 g (720 kcal)
- **Carbs**: (3200 − 640 − 720) / 4 = **460 g**

## Protein Requirements

### Current Research Consensus

| Population | Protein Target |
|------------|----------------|
| General muscle building | 1.6-2.2 g/kg/day |
| Optimal for trained lifters | 2.0-2.4 g/kg/day |
| Contest prep / cutting | 2.3-3.1 g/kg of lean body mass |
| General RDA (non-athletes) | 0.8 g/kg/day (inadequate for training) |

**Per-Meal Dose**: 0.4-0.5 g/kg bodyweight (20-40 g high-quality protein)
**Meal Frequency**: 3-6 meals spaced 3-5 hours apart
**Pre-Sleep**: 30-40 g casein for overnight muscle protein synthesis

## Carb Cycling & Fat Guidelines

### Carb Cycling Structure
- **High-carb days** (heavy training): 4.4-5.5 g/kg, 50%+ calories, lower fat
- **Medium-carb days** (moderate): ~3.5-4.0 g/kg
- **Low-carb days** (rest): ~1.1 g/kg, 20-25% calories, higher fat

Keep protein constant daily. Align high-carb with intense sessions (legs, back).

### Pre/Intra/Post-Workout Nutrition
- **Pre-workout** (1-3h before): 0.5-1.0 g/kg complex carbs
- **Intra-workout**: Unnecessary unless >90 min sessions
- **Post-workout**: The rigid 30-60min "anabolic window" is debunked
  - Actual window: **4-6 hours around training** (Aragon & Schoenfeld 2013)
  - If protein consumed 1-2h pre-workout, no urgency post-workout

### Minimum Fat for Health
- **15-20%** of total calories
- **Absolute minimum**: 0.5 g/kg/day
- Dropping below 20% can reduce testosterone

## Training Program Design

### Optimal Weekly Volume
- **10-20 sets per muscle group per week**
- **Per-session ceiling**: ~6-8 hard sets per muscle
- Volume increases: ~20% increments across mesocycles
- Relationship is logarithmic (diminishing returns beyond 20 sets)

### Rep Ranges
- **Primary hypertrophy**: 6-12 reps
- **All rep ranges work**: 6-30+ reps build muscle if near failure
- **"Effective reps"**: Last ~5 reps near failure maximally recruit motor units

### Rest Periods
- **Compound sets**: 2-3+ minutes
- **Isolation**: 60-90 seconds
- Longer rest = greater volume = more growth

### Training Frequency
- **Minimum**: 2× per week per muscle group
- **Optimal**: 2-3× per week
- Superior hypertrophy vs 1× per week (Schoenfeld 2016 meta-analysis)

## RPE/RIR System

**RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) = 10 − RIR (Reps in Reserve)**

### Targets for Hypertrophy
- **RPE 7-9** (1-3 RIR)
- **Compounds**: 2 RIR
- **Isolation**: 1 RIR
- **Limit RPE 10 (failure)** to final sets of isolation only
- Avoid failure on complex compounds to manage fatigue

**RPE accuracy requires 4-8 weeks of practice.**

## Strength Training Programs

### StrongLifts 5×5 (Beginners, 3 days/week)
- **Workout A**: Squat 5×5, Bench 5×5, Row 5×5
- **Workout B**: Squat 5×5, OHP 5×5, Deadlift 1×5
- Alternate Mon/Wed/Fri
- Start with empty bar
- Add **+5 lb per session** (+10 lb deadlift)
- **Deload**: Fail same weight 3 sessions → reduce 10%

### Wendler 5/3/1 (Intermediate+, Training Max = 90% 1RM)

| Week | Set 1 | Set 2 | Set 3 (AMRAP) |
|------|-------|-------|---------------|
| Week 1 (5s) | 5 @ 65% | 5 @ 75% | 5+ @ 85% |
| Week 2 (3s) | 3 @ 70% | 3 @ 80% | 3+ @ 90% |
| Week 3 (5/3/1) | 5 @ 75% | 3 @ 85% | 1+ @ 95% |

- **Progression**: +5 lb upper / +10 lb lower per cycle
- **Deload**: Every 7th week
- **BBB variation**: Add 5×10 at 50-70% TM for size

### GZCL Method (Intermediate)
- **T1** (85-100%, 5×3): Primary compound, strength focus
- **T2** (65-85%, 3×10): Supporting compound, volume
- **T3** (<65%, 3×15+): Isolation, hypertrophy
- 1:2:3 volume ratio
- On stall: change rep scheme (5×3 → 6×2 → 10×1) before deloading

### PPL Split (Push/Pull/Legs)
Best run 6 days/week for 2× frequency per muscle
- **Push**: Chest/Shoulders/Triceps
- **Pull**: Back/Biceps/Rear Delts
- **Legs**: Quads/Hamstrings/Glutes/Calves

### Upper/Lower Split
4 days/week (U/L/off/U/L/off/off), each muscle 2×/week
Best for intermediates with limited time

## Periodization Models

### Linear Periodization
- Phase 1: Hypertrophy (4 weeks, 3-4×10-12 @ 60-70%)
- Phase 2: Strength (4 weeks, 3-5×5-6 @ 75-85%)
- Phase 3: Peak (2-3 weeks, 3-5×1-3 @ 85-95%)
- Deload
- **Best for**: Beginners

### Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP)
- Mon: Hypertrophy (3×8-10 @ 60-75%)
- Wed: Strength (4×5 @ 80-90%)
- Fri: Power (5×1-3 @ 90-100%)
- **Best for**: Intermediates competing frequently

### Block Periodization
- Accumulation (3-6 weeks, high volume)
- Transmutation (3-4 weeks, high intensity)
- Realization (1-3 weeks, max expression)
- **Best for**: Advanced athletes preparing for competition

**Research consensus (2015 meta-analysis):** Linear, block, and undulating are **equally effective** when volume/intensity equated.

## Progressive Overload Methods

1. **Linear weight progression** (beginners): Add weight every session
2. **Double progression** (intermediates): Work within rep range (3×8-12), add reps until top, then increase weight
3. **Volume progression**: Increase sets over mesocycles (10 → 12 → 14 → 16 weekly sets)
4. **Density progression**: Same work in less time (reduce rest periods)

## Exercise Selection by Muscle

- **Chest**: Barbell Bench → Incline DB Press → Cable Fly
- **Back**: Barbell Row → Pull-Ups → Seated Cable Row
- **Shoulders**: OHP → Lateral Raise → Face Pull
- **Quads**: Back Squat → Leg Press → Bulgarian Split Squat
- **Hamstrings**: RDL → Lying Leg Curl → Nordic Curl
- **Glutes**: Hip Thrust → Deep Squat → Cable Pull-Through
- **Arms**: Close-Grip Bench / Barbell Curl → Pushdown / Incline DB Curl

**Five fundamental patterns**: Hip hinge, Squat, Horizontal/Vertical push, Horizontal/Vertical pull, Carry

## Recovery & Deload Protocols

### Deload Frequency
- Beginners: Every 8-12 weeks
- Intermediates: Every 4-6 weeks
- Advanced: Every 3-4 weeks

### Deload Methods
- **Volume deload** (recommended): Reduce sets by 40-50%, maintain weight
- **Intensity deload**: Reduce weight 40-50%, maintain sets/reps
- **5/3/1 deload**: 3 sets at 40/50/60% of training max

### Overtraining Signs
- Persistent strength decreases across multiple sessions
- Chronic fatigue not resolved by rest
- Sleep disturbances
- Elevated resting heart rate
- RPE creep (same weights feel harder)
- Soreness lasting >72 hours regularly

**Sleep**: **7-9 hours** is optimal for muscle growth. Non-negotiable.

## Body Recomposition

Works best for:
- Beginners (<1 year training)
- Detrained lifters returning after break
- Higher body fat (>20% men, >30% women)

**Protocol:**
- Eat at maintenance or slight deficit (0-20% below TDEE)
- High protein (2.0-2.4 g/kg/day)
- Strength train 3-6 days/week

For advanced lifters, traditional bulk/cut cycles likely more effective.

## Tracking & Progress Measurement

### Metrics to Track
- **Weight**: Daily morning (fasted, post-bathroom), use **weekly averages**
- **Body measurements**: Biweekly (chest, waist, arms, thighs)
- **Lifts**: Every session — progressive overload is #1 indicator
- **Photos**: Monthly (same lighting, angles, time)
- **Subjective**: Energy, mood, sleep

### Adjustment Rules
- Track **2-3 weeks** before making changes
- No weight change after 2-3 weeks → adjust by 100-200 kcal/day
- Recalculate TDEE every 4-6 weeks or after 10-15 lbs change

### Rate of Weight Change Targets

| Goal | Rate |
|------|------|
| Lean bulk (novice/intermediate) | 0.25-0.5% BW/week |
| Lean bulk (advanced) | ≤0.25% BW/week |
| Fat loss (muscle-preserving) | 0.5-1.0% BW/week |
| Aggressive cut (contest prep) | Up to 1% BW/week max |

## Troubleshooting

### Signs of Undereating (Too Aggressive Deficit)
- Persistent fatigue, brain fog, irritability
- Strength declining 2+ consecutive weeks
- Loss of menstrual cycle (women)
- Excessive muscle loss (>1% BW/week)
- Chronic hunger, sleep disruption
- Hair loss, frequent illness

**Fix**: Increase calories by 200-300, prioritize protein and sleep

### Signs of Overeating (Too Aggressive Surplus)
- Gaining >1 lb/week consistently (most is fat)
- Noticeable visceral fat accumulation
- Feeling sluggish
- Elevated fasting insulin/triglycerides

**Fix**: Reduce surplus by 200 kcal, aim for 0.25-0.5% BW/week gain

### Strength Plateau Troubleshooting
1. Check recovery (sleep, nutrition, stress)
2. Check volume (too little → add sets; too much → deload)
3. Try changing rep ranges or exercises
4. Switch periodization model (linear → undulating)
5. Ensure progressive overload tracked and applied

### Common Training Mistakes
- Training too far from failure (RPE <6 = wasted sets)
- Excessive junk volume (>8 hard sets per muscle per session)
- Insufficient frequency (<2×/week per muscle)
- Neglecting progressive overload tracking
- Ego lifting with poor form
- Not deloading when fatigue accumulates

## Supplement Tier List (Evidence-Based)

### S-Tier (Strong Evidence)
- **Creatine monohydrate**: 3-5 g/day, no loading required; +5-15% strength
- **Whey protein**: 20-40 g per serving for MPS
- **Caffeine**: 3-6 mg/kg, 30-60 min pre-exercise; enhanced performance

### A-Tier (Good Evidence)
- **Beta-alanine**: 3.2-6.4 g/day, divided doses; improves 60-240s work
- **Citrulline malate**: 6-8 g pre-workout; may improve reps to failure
- **Casein**: 30-40 g pre-sleep for overnight MPS

### B-Tier (Supportive)
- **Omega-3**: 1-3 g EPA/DHA daily; anti-inflammatory
- **Vitamin D**: 1,000-5,000 IU/day targeting 30-50 ng/mL
- **Magnesium**: 200-400 mg/day citrate or glycinate

### Does NOT Work
- BCAAs/leucine standalone (redundant if protein adequate)
- Turkesterone (no meaningful human evidence)
- Glutamine (no muscle-building benefit)
- Testosterone "boosters" (tribulus, D-aspartic acid)
- Commercial fat burners (negligible)
- Creatine gummies (typically underdosed)
